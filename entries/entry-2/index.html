CORE I-INTERACTION

INGREDIENTS

Chicken for stew, Various types of mushrooms, Salt, Scallions, Ginger, Cooking wine, Any kind of boiled vegetables

Chicken Soup Noodle

Pressure Cooker Version

菌菇鸡汤线面

THIS DISH IS RECOMMENDED BY WENDY WANG

Wendy grew up eating extra-thin thread noodles, a quick-cooking option perfect for breakfast. These noodles absorb broth fast, almost multiplying as you eat. They can be prepared in chicken soup or stir-fried. Originating in the Tang Dynasty, they're known for their "hair-thin silk" texture. Traditional craftsmanship has dwindled due to modernization, though handmade versions remain the best.

STEP 1: Chop the prepared chicken into pieces and rinse with water.

STEP 2: Place the chicken in cold water and add ginger and cooking wine to deodorize. When the water boils, skim off the froth in the water.

STEP 3: Place the chicken and yam in a pressure cooker and add water until all ingredients are covered.

STEP 4: Add the soaked assorted mushrooms.

STEP 5: Turn on the pressure cooker soup mode, it will take about 15-30 minutes, depending on the amount of ingredients.

STEP 6: The chicken soup part is done.

STEP 7: Bring the water to a boil, add the noodles, and remove them after 30 seconds. (You could put in some veggies at the same time, I added some spinach)

STEP 8: Place the noodles at the bottom of the bowl, arrange the vegetables, and pour the chicken broth from the pressure cooker into the bowl.
