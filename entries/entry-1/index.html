INGREDIENTS

Egg, Black tea, Soy sauce, Rock sugar, Salt, Cooking wine, Bay leaves, Star anise, Dried chili, Peppercorns, Cinnamon

TEA LEAVES+EGG=TEA EGG

Required Rice Cooker

茶叶+鸡蛋=茶叶蛋

THIS DISH ISRECOMMENDED BY VIVIAN XIAO

Vivian, hailing from Zhejiang, China, grew up enjoying tea eggs with their rich, aromatic flavor and slightly salty outer skin. In New York, she missed this taste but successfully recreated it with a simple recipe search. Tea eggs, a traditional Chinese snack, were first invented by the Han Chinese, and Yuan Mei, a Qing Dynasty gourmet, documented their preparation in detail in his book "Food List in the Garden of Sui-Yuan."

STEP 1: Boil water, add a pinch of salt, and gently place eggs in it. Cook until fully done.

STEP 2: Immediately pour the hard-boiled eggs into a bowl of cool water. Let the eggs sit in the cool water for a few minutes .

STEP 3: Remove the eggs from the cool water and gently crack the shells all overusing a spoon, ensuring a nice pattern is created.

STEP 4: Add the seasonings as follows: cooking wine, soy sauce, rock sugar, bay leaves, star anise, cinnamon, dried chili.

STEP 5: Add some black tea.

STEP 6: Ensure the eggs are fully submerged by adding water.

STEP 7: Press the rice cooker button to start cooking the eggs for about 40 minutes.

STEP 8: Once cooked, switch the rice cooker to the "keep warm" setting and let the eggs soak in the flavorful liquid for 2 hours. For even better flavor infusion, you can choose to soak the eggs overnight.
